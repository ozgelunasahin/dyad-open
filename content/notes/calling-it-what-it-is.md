# Calling It What It Is

We're 90s kids. Just old enough to remember the *before*.

Before most of life moved behind glass. Before our attention and mindspace were claimed as raw material, reframed with a profit motive and mined. Before every quiet moment was filled with a scroll.

We felt the shift.

A shift carefully engineered for a very narrow idea of worth and value. A handful of platforms discovered a wildly profitable business model at a time when most of us were digitally illiterate.

The reason why great conversations have become rare is the same reason we feel isolated, distracted, and overall, unsettled with the direction we seem to be heading towards.

The technologies that take up so much space in our lives weren't built to serve us or our interests but the few behind the algorithms.

This realization led us toward [[islands-of-coherence]].
