# Alternatives Make Change Happen

With that clarity, we set out to build an alternative. Not just another tool to meet a need, but a philosophy made tangible. A piece of soft technology that notices, cares, and respects what it is to be human.

From there, a curated network began to take shape. We started in Berlin, and quickly grew into a community of over a thousand people. We met in groups and talked.

We learned a lot by joining each conversation people had through Dyad.

How people opened up with those they had just met. How every person carried a unique way of thinking, a tone, a texture. And how certain conditions made space for the kinds of conversations we most cherish.

So we kept iterating.

In a year's time, we went from an ugly prototype to a spacious, minimal web application, now in the hands of our first circles of users.

Much has shifted since the start. In us, in the product, in what we thought we were building.

But a few things have never changed.

Our belief that I need you, and you need me to gain perspective and come near to truth. That the tools we build and use should respect our sovereignty of mind, of pace, of data. And that being with difference, in thought, perspective, experience, is how we engage more fully with life.
